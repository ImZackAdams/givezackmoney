How Does Zack Do It?

Zack's methods and processes are a unique blend of traditional learning, improvisation, and sheer dumb luck. Let's delve into the unparalleled wisdom of Zack's web development techniques:

  • Trial and Error:
    Zack firmly believes that errors aren't mistakes; they're just alternate outcomes. And if you try enough times, one of those outcomes has to be right... right?
  • Copy-Paste from StackOverflow:
    A method as ancient as time itself. Zack's motto? "Why reinvent the wheel when someone on the internet has probably done it already?"
  • The Rubber Duck Technique:
    Talking to a rubber duck about your coding problems. Zack swears by this method, although he often wonders why the duck never talks back.
  • Blame the Tools:
    If something goes wrong, it's probably the code editor's fault. Or the browser's. Or the computer's. Definitely not Zack's.
